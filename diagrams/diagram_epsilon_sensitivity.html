ε-Decay Sensitivity — Why Off-Policy MARL Lives on a Razor's Edge
Core insight: QMIX is off-policy — exploration must be managed externally through ε-greedy decay. In a cooperative sparse-reward task, the linear decay rate λ controls how fast ε drops from 1.0 to its minimum — and this single scalar determines whether training converges at all. Only a narrow range of λ values produced successful convergence. Adjacent values fail completely — not partially, not slowly, but categorically. This is not a smooth tradeoff; it is a phase transition.

Why Off-Policy Transfers This Burden to λ
On-policy methods (MAPPO, PPO) use entropy regularization and advantage estimation to implicitly balance exploration — the algorithm manages exploration pressure automatically through policy gradients.

QMIX is off-policy: the replay buffer stores transitions from an ε-greedy behavioral policy, but the agent is trained toward a greedy target policy. All exploration is externally managed through ε(t). There is no internal mechanism to detect that Q-values are insufficiently mature — the decay schedule runs regardless of learning progress.

This is architecturally efficient (no entropy terms, no clip ratios) but creates a structural vulnerability: a cooperative task with sparse rewards requires precisely timed exploration that a fixed schedule cannot adaptively guarantee.
